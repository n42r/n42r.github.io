With over a decade of experience and a recently completed PhD, my career has spanned various roles encompassing research and development, product-driven initiatives, architecture, and leadership responsibilities ([résumé](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/n42r/n42r.github.io/main/assets/pdf/kasrin-resume.pdf)).

I began my journey as a software developer, developing AI players for games and transitioned to research and development working at the intersection of technology and business strategy, focusing on emerging technologies and industry standards. Over time, my passion for product development grew, and I transitioned into roles that allowed me to serve as a pivotal link, coordinating efforts between technical, strategic business, and marketing teams within small-to-medium sized startup-like organization. I also led the technical team during this phase. For an overview of the projects I was involved in this period, I invite you to review my [project portfolio]((https://raw.githubusercontent.com/n42r/n42r.github.io/main/assets/pdf/kasrin_protfolio_upgraded_23.pdf)).

In a short but significant stage of my career, I embarked on a valuable detour as a lecturer with managerial responsibilities—reporting to the university president—in a challenging environment, particularly in war-torn Yemen in 2015. This experience further broadened my skill set and reinforced my ability to navigate complex situations while maintaining a commitment to leadership.

More recently, I have dedicated myself to pursuing my doctoral degree, drawing inspiration from my involvement in an EU manufacturing project that featured prominent companies such as Airbus, in which I lead the development of a data management software suite (source code: [github.com/simutool](https://github.com/simutool)). At the University of Bamberg in Germany, my PhD research focused on developing a novel data management architecture—a network of data catalogs—for a next generation data architecture such as data mesh and other distributed data management eco-systems. You can have a look at the [abstract of my PhD](https://n42r.github.io/phd) for more information.

My strengths lie at the intersection of product development, research and development, architecture, and organizational management. So much value is lost at the interfaces, around the corners, between activites/systems/perspectives (eg. technical, product, strategy) and it requires both imagination and leadership to explore those invisible areas, and I believe that this is one of my strengths. I am intrinsically motivated and intuitively driven, consistently seeking new opportunities to learn and grow.
