Software and technology have become so complex that we need new approaches to tackle them. That's why I have built myself as someone who can combine things holistically and connect the dots. This has resulted in a unique combination of maker/manager skills. I'm a maker as in engineering design, R&D innovation, and software architecture, and a manager as in product, people, and cross-functional leadership. 

I started out as a programmer with interest in game development (Crystal Space, anyone?) and robotics/logic-based AI, and then moved to the intersection of R&D, product management, and technical, first in mobile app development and then in data management. To give an example of how I approach things holistically, in my PhD I combined (1) my experience building data management solutions, with (2) my background in knowledge representation and reasoning, and (3) the insights I gained designing software architectures for mobile apps, to build an [architectural pattern](https://n42r.github.io/phd) for inter-organizational data cataloguing and exchange (similar to data mesh and data space).

My strengths are not solely analytical: I excel at aligning cross-functional teams under a product-oriented vision. Personally, I'm an enthusiastic person when it comes to projects and subjects that interest me. This has worked to my advantage in past leadership roles. I have also lived and worked in three continents, which has led me to become multicultural, adaptable, and more resilient.
