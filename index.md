It has always been my view that software and technology have become so complex and multi-disciplinary that we need new approaches to tackle them. Therefore, my aim has been to develop myself to someone who can combine things holistically and connect the dots. This has resulted in developing a unique combination of maker/manager skills: I'm a maker as in engineering design, R&D innovation, and software architecture, and a manager as in product and people leadership as well as cross-functional coordination. 

I started out as a programmer with interest in game development and robotics/logic-based AI, and moved to the intersection of product management, R&D, and technical, first in mobile app development and then in data management. To give an example of how I approach things holistically, in my PhD I combined (1) my experience building data management solutions, with (2) my background in knowledge representation and reasoning, and (3) the insights I gained designing software architectures for mobile apps, to build an [architectural pattern for inter-organizational data cataloguing and exchange](https://n42r.github.io/phd).

My strengths are not only technical: I excel at aligning and coordinating cross-functional teams under product-oriented goals.

Personally, I'm an enthusiastic person when it comes to projects and subjects that interest me, this has worked to my advantage in past in leadership roles when it rubbed off positively on others creating shared motivation and excitement. I have lived and worked in three continents, this has led me to becoming multicultural, adaptable, and resilient.
