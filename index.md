Nasr is the name. I tend to build products with an R&D edge and an eye on conceptual simplicity. My strengths lie at the intersection between business strategy, product design/development, and [application architecture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Applications_architecture). 

In my PhD, I proposed a novel [data architecture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data_architecture) for inter-organizational data sharing and exchange (something in between a data lake and a data mesh but for multiple organizations). My approach is *metadata-driven*: data is exchanged via 'metadata packets.' My proposal includes (1) a specification of an exchange format (the packets), (2) an identity framework (that overcomes some problems in W3C's URIs), and (3) a metadata mapping library.

Before that, I worked on product design and development of social media apps and games, and in classical AI (not machine learning). I have also spent some time doing teaching and managerial roles in universities.
