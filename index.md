It has always been my view that software and technology have become so complex and multi-disciplinary that we need new approaches to tackle them. Therefore, my aim has been to develop myself to someone who can combine things holistically and connect the dots. I started out as a programmer with interest in game development and robotics/AI, and moved to the intersection of product management, R&D, and technical, with most recently a PhD in the subject of data architcture (here's a [short abstract](https://n42r.github.io/phd)).

To give an example of how I approach things holistically, in my PhD I combined (1) my experience in data management, with (2) my background in knowledge representation and reasoning, and (3) the insights I gained designing software architectures for mobile apps, to design an *architectural pattern* for inter-organizational data cataloguing and exchange. My strengths are not only technical. I have excelled in the past in aligning and coordinating cross-functional teams under a common goal in a small to medium sized organization.

Personally, I have lived and worked in three continents, my experiences has developed me into a multicultural, adaptable, and resilient person.
