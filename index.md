# Professional Bio

My journey into engineering began with inspiration from my father's footsteps, but it's the fusion of my mother's artistic sensibility and dedication to excellence that truly defines my approach, shaping both my hobbies and career pursuits. A glance at my hobbies reveals this unique blend—from tinkering with automobiles in high school to crafting intricate arabesque designs and, most recently, constructing sound systems. This fusion of calculation and intuition has propelled me through a career marked by unexpected achievements, driven by a vision that defies conventional labels.


Transitioning from electrical engineering to computer science, I discovered a natural affinity for programming. Within just two introductory programming courses, a colleague and I developed a 20,000-line Othello game project (Java/Java3D), soaring to the top ranks in a faculty-wide competition of over 500 students. This success led to a lecture opportunity at the age of 19, inspiring future students to embrace innovative technologies. As my undergraduate years progressed, I engaged in diverse projects—from involvement in an 8-person team to build a maze-navigating robot toy car showcased at the annual university science fair, to writing term papers tackling complex technical-philosophical subjects with meticulous attention to detail.


Propelled by these successes, I delved into the realm of Artificial Intelligence before the machine learning boom. My achievements spanned a wide spectrum of foundational AI subjects (cognitive science, robotics, and logic) and various achievements: from publishing 3 papers in esteemed conferences (ECAI, KI) to supervising teams for the International RoboCup Competition.  I solidified my expertise in this burgeoning field in my master’s project where I built a novel method to equip robots with short-term memory and another to help robots deal with visually confusing illusions.

My prowess in AI opened doors to a role as a Research and Development engineer at TayaIT, where I revitalized their iOS app Tawla with advanced AI players, rapidly elevating user ratings from 3 to 4.5 (out of 5). Transitioning to software solution architecture and product strategy, I led projects such as the E-greeting card social network (Greetings Studio), driving a tenfold increase in user engagement. Streamlining cross-functional collaboration and providing strategic advice, I optimized feedback-development cycles by 25% and saved over 10-man months of valuable time and resources for the company.


Recognizing my passion for innovative product development, I pursued a PhD in applied computer science and information systems. Leading the data solutions team in a €3.5 million publicly funded manufacturing project (SIMUTOOL, EC Grant agreement ID: 680569), we developed a dynamic data lake ecosystem aimed at optimizing data asset sharing among heterogeneous systems and companies. This solution reduced turnaround time by 30% and data discovery time by 50%, fostering efficiency and collaboration across technical and social systems.


Building on the expertise I gained in the SIMUTOOL project, I conceived of a vision for a next generation data cataloging and exchange paradigm (architectural pattern) and decided to carry out my doctoral work laying the groundwork for this vision. I invented The Basin Network, recognized with a PhD with distinction and two published papers and plans for spinning this work into a future open standard for data cataloging and exchange.

My ongoing ventures include exploring new technologies like Large Language Models (LLMs) and developing passion projects in open-source music management and recommendation software. Additionally, I'm collaborating on future ventures to build intelligent food recipe services and legal advisors, with market potential in the millions.


In envisioning the future of digital technology, I anticipate its relentless complexity and the necessity to reevaluate traditional work methodologies. What's crucial is cultivating a cadre of interdisciplinary thinkers capable of connecting disparate concepts and embracing both depth and breadth of understanding. Unfortunately, such individuals are scarce, as contemporary culture often favors specialization. However, software development diverges from this trend, as evidenced by the efficacy of lean and iterative approaches. Thus, I advocate for a progressive cultural shift that values holistic perspectives. Since my undergraduate years, I've been honing my skills to contribute to this transformation, bridging the realms of engineering and leadership to drive innovation and adaptability.
