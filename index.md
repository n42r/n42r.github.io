Software and technology have become so complex that we need new approaches to tackle them. 

This has been my mission for a long time which has led me to develop a combination of strengths that do not fit into the maker-or-manager pigeonhole. I'm a maker as in tinkerer, architect, software engineer, researcher; and a manager as in product, people, and cross-functional leadership.

I started out in robotics/AI and game development (Crystal Space, anyone?) and then moved on to the intersection of R&D and product design: first in mobile app development and then in data management. I just completed a PhD where I proposed a novel [architectural pattern](phd) for cross-organizational data cataloguing (related: data mesh, data lake).

Having lived and worked in three continents has led me to become multicultural, resilient, and highly adaptable. Personally, I'm a laid-back person with a fine-tuned critical apparatus and a unique combination of passion/beginner-mindset on one side and strategic vision on the other.
